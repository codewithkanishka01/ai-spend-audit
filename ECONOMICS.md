# Economics

### What is a converted lead worth to Credex?
Let's assume Credex acts as a reseller or broker for AI credits, taking a standard 10% margin on the credits sold.
If an average startup identified by this tool is spending $2,000/month ($24,000/year) on AI infrastructure and tools, and Credex can capture that entire spend by selling them consolidated credits:
- **Revenue per user:** $24,000/year.
- **Gross Margin (10%):** $2,400/year.
Therefore, a *converted* lead (a signed customer) is worth **$2,400 per year (LTV of 1 year)** to Credex.

### What is CAC at each channel from your GTM plan?
- **Hacker News (Organic):** $0. (Time spent writing the post: ~2 hours = $100 equivalent).
- **Cold Outbound on LinkedIn:** Assuming using tools like Apollo.io ($50/mo) and sending 500 messages. 2% conversion to a booked call = 10 calls. 20% close rate = 2 customers. CAC = $25.
- **Accounting Firm Partnerships:** $0 upfront, but requires a revenue share (e.g., giving the firm 20% of our margin). CAC = $480 per acquired customer.

### What conversion rate from "audit completed" -> "consultation booked" -> "credit purchase" makes this profitable?
Because the organic CAC is near zero, almost any conversion rate is profitable. However, considering sales rep time ($100/call):
- Let's say 1,000 audits are completed.
- 5% book a consultation = 50 calls. (Cost of sales time = $5,000).
- If the close rate from consultation to purchase is 10% = 5 customers.
- 5 customers * $2,400 margin = $12,000 in gross margin.
- **Profit:** $12,000 - $5,000 = $7,000.
**Conclusion:** A 5% booking rate and a 10% close rate (0.5% total conversion from audit to purchase) makes this highly profitable.

### What would have to be true for this tool to drive $1M ARR in 18 months?
To generate $1M ARR in margin, Credex needs:
- $1M / $2,400 margin per customer = **417 converted customers.**
If the conversion rate from "Audit Completed" to "Customer" is 0.5%, we need:
- 417 / 0.005 = **83,400 total completed audits in 18 months.**
That is ~4,600 audits per month.
For this to be true, the tool must go viral beyond a single Hacker News post. It needs a sustained growth loop (e.g., the shareable URL becoming a standard benchmark startups share with investors: "Look how optimized our AI spend is").
