# Go-To-Market Strategy

### Who is the exact target user
The target user is a **Founder or CTO at an early-stage or growth-stage startup** (Seed to Series B). They have a team of 5-50 engineers and marketers, and they recently authorized corporate cards for various AI tools but have lost track of the aggregate spend. They are technically competent enough to understand API vs retail pricing but too busy to constantly monitor the pricing pages of 10 different vendors.

### What they Google or scroll through right before they'd want this tool
They are likely scrolling through **Hacker News** discussions about "Claude 3.5 Sonnet vs GPT-4o pricing" or searching "Cursor enterprise pricing vs GitHub Copilot". They might also be reviewing their monthly Ramp or Brex statement and wincing at the $1,500 "OpenAI" line item without knowing who exactly is using it.

### Where they hang out online
- **Subreddits:** r/SaaS, r/startups, r/devops, r/ExperiencedDevs.
- **Slack/Discord:** YC Startup School Slack, local tech hub Slack communities (e.g., Techstars alumni), IndieHackers.
- **X (Twitter):** Following accounts like Gergely Orosz (Pragmatic Engineer), swyx, or VCs tweeting about AI infrastructure costs.

### How to get the first 100 users in 30 days with $0 paid budget
1. **The "Show HN" Launch:** Launch on Hacker News with the title "Show HN: I built a tool to show startups exactly how much they are overpaying for AI". HN loves cost-optimization tools and bashing bloated SaaS pricing. This is the primary driver.
2. **Cold Outreach to "Just Raised" Startups:** Scrape Crunchbase for startups that raised Seed rounds in the last 3 months. Cold DM the CTOs on LinkedIn: "Hey [Name], I noticed you recently raised. Startups your size usually waste $500/mo on overlapping AI tools (Cursor + Copilot + ChatGPT). I built a free calculator to audit your stack in 30 seconds."
3. **Engineering Newsletters:** Reach out to writers of popular dev newsletters (like ByteByteGo or Pragmatic Engineer) offering the tool as a free resource they can share with their audience.

### The unfair distribution channel
**Accounting/Bookkeeping firms for startups.** Firms like Pilot or Kruze Consulting manage the books for thousands of startups. We could partner with them to offer the "AI Spend Audit" as a value-add report they send to their clients during month-end close. Credex gets highly qualified leads, and the accounting firm looks proactive about saving their clients money.

### What week-1 traction looks like if this works
- **Day 1 (Launch):** 1,500 unique visitors from Hacker News. 300 audits completed.
- **Lead Capture:** 15% conversion rate on audits >$500 savings to email capture = 45 emails.
- **Referrals:** 10 of those users share their public URL with their co-founders via Slack.
- **End of Week 1:** ~80 highly qualified leads in the database for Credex to contact.
